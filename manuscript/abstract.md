# Abstract

**Deep Learning Super-Resolution for MRI: Technical Advances and Translational Potential for Low-Resource Settings**

Magnetic resonance imaging remains inaccessible to most of the world's population, particularly in low- and middle-income countries where scanner availability, field strength, and technical expertise are severely limited. Deep learning super-resolution (SR) offers a promising pathway to enhance image quality from low-cost, low-field MRI systems, yet the translational readiness of current methods for resource-constrained settings is unknown. We conducted a systematic review of 56 studies published between 2020 and 2025 that apply deep learning SR to MRI. Convolutional neural networks dominate (46%), with brain MRI as the primary target anatomy (48%). Reviewed methods achieve a median PSNR of 34.1 dB and SSIM of 0.913. Critically, only 27% of studies reference low-field MRI, 16% share source code, and just 34% score high on our novel LMIC-relevance framework. We identify key gaps in domain adaptation, computational efficiency, and open science practices that must be addressed to unlock the global health potential of MRI super-resolution.
